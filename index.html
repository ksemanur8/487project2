An Introduction to Studio Ghibli

Studio Ghibli was founded in 1985 in Tokyo by Isao Takahata (screenwriter, director, producer), Toshio Suzuki (producer), and Hayao Miyazaki (animator, director, producer, screenwriter, author, manga artist). The name Studio Ghibli was based on the Arabic name for "hot sahara wind" because the founders wanted Studio Ghibli to blow new wind through the anime industry.

At the time of Studio Ghibli's founding, anime was looked down upon in the film industry. The breakthrough for Studio Ghibli was in 1989 with the movie Kiki's Delivery Service. After this Miyazaki and producer Suzuki continued to make many more hits after Kiki.

Studio Ghibli films continue to be beloved by children and adults alike for many reasons including the art style, non-stereotypical characters, and the way they combine magic with realism.
